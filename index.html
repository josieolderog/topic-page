Ledges State Park is renowned for its distinctive rock formations and breathtaking natural scenery. The park offers a serene retreat for outdoor enthusiasts and nature lovers, with a network of well-maintained trails that wind through rugged terrain and provide panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.
Whether you're interested in hiking, rock climbing, or simply enjoying a quiet moment immersed in nature, Ledges State Park provides an inviting and memorable experience.

Beautiful Cliffsides

Ledges State Park, located in Boone County, Iowa, is known for its stunning natural beauty, particularly its dramatic cliffsides and rock formations. The park is situated along the Des Moines River, and its landscape is shaped by the combination of river erosion and glacial activity, creating an impressive array of sandstone and shale cliffs. These cliffs rise sharply from the river valley and are scattered with deep ravines, rock outcroppings, and wooded slopes. The steep, rugged terrain offers sweeping views of the river valley below.

Scenic Trails

Ledges State Park offers a variety of scenic trails that wind through its beautiful landscapes. Whether you enjoy a relaxing walk or a challenging hike, the park has something for everyone. The trails provide breathtaking views of the cliffs, forests, and river, making them a must-see for any nature lover. Some trails include Canyon Road and Table Rock Loop from Canyon Road, Lost Lake Trail, Ledges Table Rock Trail and Inspiration Point, Crow's Nest Loop, Oak Woods, Prairie, and Hog's Back Loop, Table Rock Loop, Ledges Prairie Trail, and Ledges Hike-in Camp Trail.

Diverse Wildlife

Ledges State Park is home to a diverse array of wildlife, making it a haven for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts. The park's unique landscape—featuring dense woodlands, sandstone cliffs, and the Des Moines River—creates a variety of habitats that support many different species. Commonly spotted mammals in the park include white-tailed deer, raccoons, foxes, and opossums. If you're lucky, you might even see a bobcat or coyote roaming through the wooded areas. Small mammals like chipmunks and squirrels are frequent visitors along the park's trails. Birdwatchers will enjoy the presence of numerous species, including red-tailed hawks, barred owls, woodpeckers, and wild turkeys. During migration seasons, the park attracts a variety of songbirds, including warblers, orioles, and bluebirds. The cliffs and trees provide excellent nesting spots for these birds.
